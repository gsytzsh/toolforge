About BMI Calculator

This BMI calculator computes your Body Mass Index using the standard WHO formula and classifies the result into standard health categories. BMI is widely used as a quick screening tool for weight-related health assessment.

BMI Formula

Metric units:

BMI = weight (kg) / height (m)²

Example: 70 kg / (1.70 m)² = 70 / 2.89 = 24.2

Imperial units:

BMI = 703 × weight (lbs) / height (in)²

Example: 703 × 154 lbs / (67 in)² = 108262 / 4489 = 24.1

BMI Categories (WHO Standard)

Category BMI Range Health Risk
Underweight < 18.5 Malnutrition risk
Normal weight 18.5 - 24.9 Low risk
Overweight 25 - 29.9 Moderate risk
Obese Class I 30 - 34.9 High risk
Obese Class II 35 - 39.9 Very high risk
Obese Class III ≥ 40 Extremely high risk

Health Implications by Category

  • Underweight: May indicate malnutrition, eating disorders, or underlying health conditions. Increased risk of osteoporosis and weakened immune function.
  • Normal: Associated with lowest risk of weight-related health problems. Maintain with balanced diet and regular exercise.
  • Overweight: Increased risk of cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, and certain cancers. Lifestyle modifications recommended.
  • Obese: Significantly elevated risk of heart disease, stroke, diabetes, sleep apnea, and joint problems. Medical intervention may be beneficial.

BMI Limitations

While useful for population-level screening, BMI has important limitations:

  • Doesn't measure body composition: Cannot distinguish between muscle and fat mass
  • Athletes: May be classified as overweight/obese due to high muscle mass
  • Elderly: May underestimate fat due to muscle loss with age
  • Ethnicity: Health risks may vary at same BMI across different ethnic groups
  • Fat distribution: Doesn't account for visceral fat (more dangerous than subcutaneous)

Better Health Indicators

For comprehensive health assessment, consider alongside BMI:

  • Waist circumference: > 40" (men) or > 35" (women) indicates higher risk
  • Body fat percentage: More accurate measure of body composition
  • Blood pressure: Key indicator of cardiovascular health
  • Blood markers: Cholesterol, blood sugar, inflammation markers
  • Fitness level: Cardiorespiratory fitness is independent health predictor

Frequently Asked Questions

What is BMI and how is it calculated?
BMI (Body Mass Index) is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. Formula: BMI = weight (kg) / height (m)². For imperial units: BMI = 703 × weight (lbs) / height (in)². Results categorize as underweight, normal, overweight, or obese.
What are the BMI categories?
WHO BMI categories: Underweight (< 18.5), Normal weight (18.5-24.9), Overweight (25-29.9), Obese Class I (30-34.9), Obese Class II (35-39.9), Obese Class III (≥ 40). Categories apply to adults 20+ years.
What are the limitations of BMI?
BMI doesn't distinguish muscle from fat, so athletes may be classified overweight despite low body fat. It doesn't account for bone density, age, sex, or fat distribution. BMI is a screening tool, not a diagnostic measure of health.
Is BMI different for men and women?
No, BMI categories are the same for adult men and women. However, women naturally have higher body fat percentage than men at the same BMI. BMI doesn't account for these physiological differences.
What is a healthy BMI range?
For most adults, a BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is considered healthy. However, optimal BMI varies by individual factors including age, muscle mass, bone density, and overall health. Consult healthcare providers for personalized guidance.
