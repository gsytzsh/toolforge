About Odd Even Lines

This tool splits text into odd-numbered lines and even-numbered lines. Line 1 is the first line.

It is useful for separating key-value pairs, alternating data, or extracting every other line from logs and exports.

How to Use Odd Even Lines

  1. Step 1: Open Odd Even Lines and enter your source input data in the main field.
  2. Step 2: Choose transformation options such as case, trimming, filtering or replacement rules.
  3. Step 3: Run processing and compare the transformed text with your expected result.
  4. Step 4: Copy cleaned text into documents, code comments or content pipelines.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Odd Even Lines mainly used for?
Odd Even Lines is mainly used to split text into odd-numbered lines (1, 3, 5…) and even-numbered lines (2, 4, 6…).
Does Odd Even Lines send my input to a server?
For normal usage, Odd Even Lines processes data locally in your browser so you can work without uploading project files.
Why does processed text differ from expectation?
Whitespace rules, hidden characters and line-ending differences are common reasons for unexpected text output.
