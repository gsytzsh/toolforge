Title Case Rules

Standard title case capitalization follows these rules:

  • Capitalize: First and last words, nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, pronouns
  • Lowercase: Articles (a, an, the), coordinating conjunctions (and, but, or), prepositions (in, on, at, by) unless they're the first word
Input:  "the quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog"
Title:  "The Quick Brown Fox Jumps Over the Lazy Dog"

Note: "the" and "over" are lowercase (preposition/article in middle)

Readability Tips

  • Avoid long UPPERCASE passages: Studies show all-caps text is 13-20% slower to read
  • Use Title Case sparingly: Best for short titles, not body text
  • Sentence case is most readable: Default choice for paragraphs and descriptions
  • Mix cases for emphasis: Strategic UPPERCASE draws attention to key points

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the different text case formats?
Common case formats: UPPERCASE (all caps), lowercase (all small), Title Case (Capitalize Each Word), Sentence case (First letter only), camelCase (firstLowerThenUpper), snake_case (under_scores). Each has specific use cases.
When should I use UPPERCASE?
UPPERCASE is used for: emphasis or headings, acronyms (NASA, FBI), constants in code (MAX_VALUE), warnings/alarms, and button labels. Avoid long passages as they're harder to read.
What is Title Case and when is it used?
Title Case capitalizes the first letter of major words. Used for: book/movie titles, article headlines, section headings. Minor words (a, an, the, and, but, or) are typically lowercase unless first word.
What is Sentence case?
Sentence case capitalizes only the first word and proper nouns, like standard writing. Used for: body text, descriptions, captions, and most prose. It's the default case for readable content.
Why is text case important in programming?
Most programming languages are case-sensitive. Variable names, function names, and keywords must match exactly. Following language conventions (camelCase, snake_case) improves code readability and maintainability.
