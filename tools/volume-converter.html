About Volume Converter

This volume converter converts between metric units (liters, milliliters, cubic meters) and imperial/US customary units (gallons, cubic feet) using precisely defined conversion factors.

It is useful for cooking recipe conversions, engineering fluid calculations, storage tank capacity planning, scientific measurements, aquarium volume calculations, and any situation requiring conversion between different volume measurement systems.

Common Conversion Formulas

Liters to Gallons:     gal = L × 0.264172
Liters to Cubic Feet:  ft³ = L × 0.0353147

Milliliters to Liters: L = mL ÷ 1000
Cubic Meters to Liters: L = m³ × 1000

US vs UK Gallons

The US and UK (Imperial) systems use different gallon definitions:

Type Liters Cubic Inches Usage
US Gallon 3.785411784 L 231 in³ USA, some Latin America
UK (Imperial) Gallon 4.54609 L 277.42 in³ UK, some Commonwealth

Note: This tool uses US gallons. For UK gallons, multiply liters by 0.219969.

  • 1 liter = 1000 milliliters = 1000 cubic centimeters

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the exact conversion factors for volume units?
The exact conversion factors are: 1 liter = 1000 milliliters = 0.001 cubic meters = 0.264172 US gallons = 0.0353147 cubic feet. 1 US gallon = 3.785411784 liters (exact). 1 cubic foot = 28.316846592 liters. These factors are based on international metric and US customary definitions.
What is the difference between US and UK gallons?
US gallon = 3.785411784 liters (231 cubic inches). UK (Imperial) gallon = 4.54609 liters (277.42 cubic inches). The UK gallon is approximately 20% larger than the US gallon. This tool uses US gallons; for UK gallons multiply liters by 0.219969.
How do I convert liters to gallons manually?
To convert liters to US gallons, divide by 3.785411784 (or multiply by 0.264172). For example: 10 L ÷ 3.785411784 = 2.64172 US gallons. To convert gallons to liters, multiply by 3.785411784.
What is a cubic meter used for?
Cubic meters (m³) are the SI unit for volume, used in scientific contexts, engineering calculations, water utility metering, natural gas measurement, and large-scale industrial applications. 1 cubic meter equals 1000 liters or approximately 264.172 US gallons.
What are common metric volume prefixes?
Metric volume uses decimal prefixes: 1 liter = 1000 milliliters (mL), 100 centiliters (cL), or 0.001 kiloliters (kL). Common conversions: 1 mL = 1 cubic centimeter (cm³), 1000 L = 1 cubic meter (m³).
When should I use fluid ounces vs dry ounces?
Fluid ounces (fl oz) measure volume for liquids (1 US fl oz = 29.5735 mL). Dry ounces measure weight/mass. For cooking, use fluid ounces for liquids (water, milk, oil) and weight ounces for dry ingredients (flour, sugar). They are not interchangeable.
