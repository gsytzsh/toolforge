About Text Statistics & Word Counter

The Text Statistics & Word Counter tool lets you count words, characters and lines in one place for writing, SEO and content analysis.

It is useful for editing copy, cleaning pasted text, comparing content and making small text transformations directly in the browser.

How to Use Text Statistics

  1. Step 1: Open Text Statistics and enter your source input data in the main field.
  2. Step 2: Choose transformation options such as case, trimming, filtering or replacement rules.
  3. Step 3: Run processing and compare the transformed text with your expected result.
  4. Step 4: Copy cleaned text into documents, code comments or content pipelines.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Text Statistics mainly used for?
Text Statistics is mainly used to count words, characters and lines in one place for writing, SEO, editing and content analysis.
Does Text Statistics send my input to a server?
For normal usage, Text Statistics processes data locally in your browser so you can work without uploading project files.
Why does processed text differ from expectation?
Whitespace rules, hidden characters and line-ending differences are common reasons for unexpected text output.
