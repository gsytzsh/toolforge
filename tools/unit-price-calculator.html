About Unit Price Calculator


This unit price calculator compares two products by cost per unit so you can see which one gives better value for the same type of item.


It is useful for grocery shopping, bulk-pack comparisons and product size decisions where the cheaper sticker price does not always mean the better deal.

Frequently Asked Questions



  
How do I calculate unit price manually?

  
Divide the total price by the quantity. For example, $5.99 for 500g = $5.99/500 = $0.01198 per gram, or $11.98 per kg.

  
Should I compare by weight or volume?

  
Compare identical units. Weight (g, oz) for solids, volume (ml, fl oz) for liquids. Never mix different unit types.

  
Does bulk always mean better value?

  
Not always. Calculate unit price to verify. Sometimes smaller packs have promotions or the bulk premium isn't worth it.

  
How do I handle different unit sizes?

  
Convert both to the same base unit first. For example, convert ounces to grams or gallons to liters before comparing.

  
What if the products have different quality?

  
Unit price compares cost only. Factor in quality, expiration dates, and your actual needs when making the final decision.

  
Is unit price useful for non-food items?

  
Yes. Use for batteries (price per cell), paper (price per sheet), cables (price per meter), and any bulk purchases.
